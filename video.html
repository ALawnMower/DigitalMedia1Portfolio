 Digital Video Editing Intro | April 13, 2021


Video editing can be a very satisfying and fulfilling digital media experience. Digital video productions of all sorts represent the majority of the traffic on the web where YouTube alone is responsible for over 1 billion hours of viewing each day. In this class we will only scratch the surface with what is possible in capturing and editing video with the goal of sampling your skills for your portfolio. Because of the multiple modes of learning in the pandemic, users are on many different platforms/environments and we are pretty open to the choice of editing software. Chromebook are not the best video editors but if its your only option WeVideo may get you started, Windows or Mac computers are recommended.

 In this introduction assignment, your tasks are pretty simple: Choose a platform for editing. Import multiple clips from your camera or stock video footage.  Slow, speed up or even reverse a clip Splice, split or trim a clip. Eliminate audio. Separate audio. Control audio levels. Add text. Add background. Import audio for sounds effects. Tell a story

 Movie Trailer | April 29, 2021 

 Movie Trailers are used to advertise upcoming movies. In this project, students may work individually or in teams to create their own movie trailer. Within the process of making a movie trailer, students learn to create advanced motion and color effects, learn audio-editing techniques, and compose a musical score. Finally, each team will have their project peer evaluated and viewed on the projector in class. 

Project guidelines: Length should be 90 seconds Include still images that incorporate transitions and effects (animators may included animated sequence) Include a musical score and/or sound effects Include an opening title sequence and full end credits Apply copyright citation and fair use guidelines where applicable Follow the Journalistic Code of Ethics
