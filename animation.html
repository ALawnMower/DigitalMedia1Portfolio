Ball Bounce II | January 27, 2021 

Introducing Tweening, Movie Symbols, and Instance Transforms 

Animate can be used to create powerful frame based animations but its real power is in tweening. A tween is a co-property of a symbol as it resides on a layer. You can place a symbol in a position on or off the stage within a single keyframe, insert another keyframe on the same layer at a later point in time and change ANY property of the symbol over time and have Animate do the animation work for you. 

Content Objectives

 Recreate the ball bounce animation with tweening instead of frame by frame animation Transform the ball on each keyframe to change size or to squish the ball as it contacts the surface it bounces from Introduce another object to intercept the ball Have background tween

Animation with Sound | March 19, 2021 

We previously created a 30 second audio using Audacity. Now, armed with skills to animate, we will import that audio into a new Flash project that will contain the following: Title and Credits Sequence (this may extend the 30 seconds of audio) Layer titled "Actions" for scripting and text labels Layer for imported audio Layers for each symbol with tweening etc... New "Shape" tweening 

 30 seconds may seem like a daunting task in the world of animation (that is 720 frames using the default frame rate.) To break this down a bit, you will want to create a storyboard inside of your author file. After importing and placing your audio, you can create a layer called "Actions" and insert keyframes with text labels mapping out the events you need to animate. Next, it is a matter of creating all of the symbols needed in each sequence. Remember, you can create small sequences and save them as "Movie Symbols" to later introduce them when needed.
